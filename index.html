THE LAST ODYSSEY

Video Submission for MDDN222

The Last Odyssey immerses you in the heart of a world forever altered by humanity’s untamed carbon emissions and the relentless onslaught of climate change.

Set in the year 5000, this project throws you into the eerie aftermath of a planet left to the mercy of its own fury. Once a thriving home to humanity, Earth now exists as an alien and perilous realm. As a member of the deteriorating space colony (the last of humankind), the player assumes the role of a brave explorer sent on a mission to Earth to access vitality .

Mission Statement ಠ_ಠ

When I was a kid I wanted to be an artist, but I was made to believe that no one can make a living selling paintings. While studying media design, I have come to understand that art encompasses much more than paintings or sculptures. Art can be found in any creative endeavour, from storytelling to advertising. My aim as a designer is to infuse each project I undertake with artistic elements, whether it is to tell a compelling story, promote a product, or share meaningful opinions. By viewing my work as an opportunity for art, I aim to transcend mere functionality and create experiences that resonate deeply with audiences.

Keep It Clean

I designed my digital portfolio with the environment in mind.

(^^ゞ The still images have all been DITHERED. Dithering is a technique for reducing image file size by introducing noise to represent colours that are not in the original palette. This approach require less data transfer, which is particularly important for mobile users and those on slower internet connections.

The site uses only DEFAULT TYPEFACES. With the exception of the arrow icons displayed in the gallery banner, this minimalist approach means relying on system fonts or widely available web fonts rather than custom or elaborate typography. The logo is simply my typed name; Ava-Jane. Default typefaces require minimal additional resources, as they are already available on your devices. This contributes to faster rendering of web pages, lower energy consumption, and a smaller carbon footprint. ヽ(^。^)ノ

ヽ(^。^)ノ This is a personal website designed solely for showcasing my work, therefore I don't need to monetize it with advertising or third-party cookies.

Keeping it all in one place. Limiting my content to a single page reduces the complexity of the website and the number of server requests required to load it.

But, Why?

Low Carbon Web Design, in my opinion, is critical to the design industry in the near future and beyond. As our world faces urgent environmental concerns, designers must accept responsibility for the digital things they make. LCWD is not only in line with sustainability objectives, but it also makes good commercial sense. It enables designers to construct websites that are more efficient, faster, while providing better user experiences. This, in turn, can lead to improved user engagement, lower operating costs, and a happier, healthier world.
